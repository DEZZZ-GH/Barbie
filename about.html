The Story Behind the Doll

Barbie was born in 1959, created by Ruth Handler and introduced by the American toy company Mattel. Her full name is Barbara Millicent Roberts, inspired by Ruth’s own daughter. When Barbie first appeared at the New York Toy Fair wearing a black-and-white swimsuit and a confident smile, she became an instant icon. She represented independence, style, and imagination at a time when most dolls were babies or caretakers. Over the decades, Barbie evolved to reflect the world around her — becoming an astronaut, a doctor, a president, and even a game developer. She's had over 250 careers and continues to inspire generations of kids to dream big and be anything.

A Style Icon Through the Decades

Fashion has always been at the heart of Barbie's world. Since her debut in 1959, Barbie has been a trendsetter — from classic 60s dresses to futuristic runway looks. With thousands of outfits created by real designers and inspired by global trends, Barbie isn’t just a doll — she’s a fashion statement. She’s walked runways, worn couture, and even collaborated with top brands like Moschino, Balmain, and Vera Wang. Whether she’s in a power suit, a glittering ballgown, or a lab coat, Barbie proves that style can be bold, playful, and limitless — just like the girls who play with her.
